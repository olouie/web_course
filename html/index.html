Creamy Potato Soup Recipe

The best creamy potato soup ever!

Makes about 6 servingss

Ingredients:

  • 1 medium onion, diced
  • 4 cloves of garlic, minced
  • 2 lbs potatoes, roughly cubed/diced to however size you want
  • 2 Tbsp oil or butter
  • 4 cups chicken broth
  • 1 cup milk or cream
  • Salth and pepper, to taste
  • Cheddar cheese, scallions, bacon, meats any toppings if desired

Directions:

  1. In a large stock pot over medium heat, sauté the onions and garlic in 2 Tbsp of oil or butter until soft.
  2. Add the cubed/diced potatoes and the broth and bring to a boil. Turn heat to low and simmer, partially covered for 10 minutes until potatoes are soft.
  3. Remove from heat and add milk or cream, and stir.
  4. Using an immersion blender, blend soup until desired consistency. Alternatively, you can pour the soup into a blender to blend. Also, you can also mash the potatoes with a potato masher if you lack fancy blending apparatuses.
  5. Salt and pepper to taste, and garnish with whatever toppings if desired.

Notes:

  • For a grainer texture use Russell potatoes.
  • The soup will thicken as the potatoes get blended. Do not blend all the potatoes if you want a chunky soup.
  • To check the doneness of the potatoes, stick a fork in a piece. If it pierces the potato fairly easily, then the potatoes are done.
  • Cut the potoes into different sized cubes. As the potatoes cook, they will break apart a little bit. The larger cubes ensures good sized chunks of potato. The smaller cubes help thicken the soup.
